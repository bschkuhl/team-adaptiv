Human-Computer Interaction Studies

Comprehensive analysis of interaction patterns between humans and AI systems in collaborative work environments.

Status: Ongoing
Duration: September 2023 - Present
Team Size: 8 Researchers

Observational study of human-AI collaboration in workplace settings

Project Overview

This ongoing research investigates how humans interact with AI systems in collaborative work environments, focusing on understanding interaction patterns, trust dynamics, and performance outcomes. Our study encompasses multiple domains including creative work, data analysis, and decision-making processes.

The project aims to inform the design of AI systems that can effectively collaborate with humans, enhancing productivity while maintaining human agency and satisfaction in the workplace.

Research Objectives

  • Analyze communication patterns between humans and AI systems
  • Investigate trust formation and maintenance in human-AI teams
  • Identify factors that influence collaborative effectiveness
  • Develop frameworks for evaluating human-AI team performance
  • Create design guidelines for collaborative AI interfaces

Methodology

Our research employs ethnographic studies, controlled experiments, and longitudinal field studies across various organizational contexts. We use mixed-methods approaches combining quantitative performance metrics with qualitative analysis of interaction dynamics.

Data collection includes video analysis of work sessions, survey instruments measuring trust and satisfaction, performance metrics, and interview studies with participants from different professional backgrounds.

Preliminary Findings

Trust Development

Trust in AI systems develops gradually over 4-6 weeks of regular interaction, with initial skepticism giving way to conditional acceptance.

Communication Patterns

Successful human-AI teams develop shared vocabularies and communication protocols that improve over time with experience.

Performance Metrics

Human-AI teams show 42% better performance on complex tasks compared to human-only teams, with highest gains in analytical work.

Key Research Areas

Trust and Transparency

Investigating how AI system transparency affects user trust and decision-making. We examine the role of explainable AI in fostering appropriate reliance on automated systems.

Collaborative Workflows

Analyzing how work processes adapt when AI systems are introduced, including changes in task allocation, decision authority, and workflow optimization.

Cognitive Integration

Studying how humans mentally model AI capabilities and limitations, and how these mental models influence interaction strategies and outcomes.

Case Studies

Creative Design Teams

12-month study of graphic design teams using AI-assisted creative tools, focusing on how AI suggestions influence creative processes and output quality.

Financial Analysis

Longitudinal study of investment analysts working with AI-powered market analysis tools, examining decision-making patterns and risk assessment accuracy.

Medical Diagnosis

Observational study of radiologists using AI diagnostic assistance, investigating how AI recommendations influence diagnostic confidence and accuracy.

Future Directions

Upcoming phases of the research will expand to include virtual and remote collaboration scenarios, investigate cultural differences in human-AI interaction patterns, and develop predictive models for team formation and performance optimization.

We are also planning to create an open dataset of human-AI interaction patterns that can be used by researchers and designers to improve collaborative AI systems.
